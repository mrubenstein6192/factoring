The most important part of factoring is NOT being afraid to be wrong and try again!
It's a bit of a "guess-and-check!"
Standard form for a quadratic expression is
ax2 + bx + c
Your goal is to find two numbers that multiply to "c" and add up to "b".
You will also have to decide between " + " and " - " for each number.
Standard form for a quadratic expression is
ax2 + bx + c
Since a > 1, factoring is a little tougher, but you can do it! Practice makes perfect!

First, think of two numbers to be placed in front of each "x" in each set of parentheses that multiply to "a."

Second, think of two numbers to be placed at the end of each set of parentheses that multiply to "c."

The last part is the toughest, but you'll get the hang of it with repetition: Think of the four numbers you chose as First, Second, Third, Fourth reading from left to right. You need (First)(Fourth) + (Second)(Third) to add up to "b."
